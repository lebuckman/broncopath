A Day in the Life
Follow Alex, a CS junior at CPP, through a typical 9:45 AM morning on campus. Each screen exists to solve a real problem she faces.
Screen 1 of 5 · Onboarding
Alex opens the app for the first time
It's Monday morning, 9:45 AM. Alex just finished her 8 AM lecture and has a 45-minute gap before her next class. She heard about BroncoPath from a friend last week and finally downloads it.

The splash screen immediately communicates what the app is for — no lengthy tutorial, no signup wall. Just a clean hook and a single action to take.
Why this screen exists
First impressions matter. The splash establishes brand trust and gives the user one clear next step. "Get Started" leads directly into value — no friction.
Screen 2 of 5 · Dashboard
The "right now" snapshot
Alex lands on the home screen. Without doing anything, she can already see that Engineering (where she's heading) is 88% full and the College of Business is nearly empty. She wonders if there's a quiet room to study in before her next class.

The quick action cards at the bottom give her two clear next moves based on what she likely wants to do right now.
Why this screen exists
Students need a glanceable summary of campus conditions without searching. The dashboard delivers context in under 3 seconds — the same way a weather app shows you the forecast the moment you open it.
Screen 3 of 5 · Campus Map
A spatial overview of the whole campus
Alex taps the Map tab. She can see the full campus layout with buildings color-coded. Green means quiet. Red means crowded. She notices the College of Business (163) is nearly empty — she taps it to check the rooms inside.

The live "You are here" pulse helps her orient herself immediately. She can also filter to show only quiet buildings.
Why this screen exists
CPP's existing campus map shows building names — nothing more. This map adds a layer of intelligence: spatial context + real-time occupancy in one view. The map is the core differentiator vs. the current tool.
Screen 4 of 5 · Find a Room
Finding a quiet spot to study
Alex taps "Find a Room." She immediately sees that 163-101 through 163-210 are all free in the Business building. She can also see which Library study rooms are open, and that some Engineering rooms free up at 10 AM.

She expands the Library accordion to confirm Study Room A is available and unbooked until 11 AM.
Why this screen exists
Students waste real time wandering buildings looking for an open room. This screen solves that friction entirely — room-level granularity means Alex knows exactly where to go before she walks there.
Screen 5 of 5 · Route Planner
Getting to class the smart way
It's 10:50 AM now. Alex needs to get from the CLA building to Engineering for her 11 AM class. She opens Routes. The app shows her two options: a 6-minute fastest route that passes through the busy Science Building corridor, or a 9-minute crowd-avoiding route that stays quieter.

She picks the recommended route. The crowd tip also tells her the fast path will be clear after 10:15 — useful for next time.
Why this screen exists
Shortest path ≠ best path on a crowded campus. This screen introduces crowd-weighted routing: the key technical innovation that no existing campus tool provides. Even a 3-minute difference matters when you're carrying a backpack through a packed hallway.
